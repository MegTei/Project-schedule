Scope
Current state materials will be benchmarked and mapped to viable closed-loop alternatives suitable for precinct long-term (evergreen) applications.
The target responsible materials will detail: a) A specific product spec (e.g. MDF 18mm, sheet size) b) evergreen precinct applications (e.g. facades) c) What it replaces d) Cost comparison to current state materials and Lifespan ROSI e) Methods
A comparative business case will be modelled, with clear recommendations embedded into RFP specifications.
Primary focus: Precinct designers and agencies - the precinct RFP is the mechanism for change

Product led vs standard approach

AO27 is a structured feasibility phase to identify which products and suppliers can reliably meet a future mandated standard.

The challenge to solve:

Product-led materials:

i) Compliance becomes fragile — if builders can't access those exact products (availability, cost, timing), they will substitute alternatives without equivalent scrutiny.

ii) It doesn't build procurement discipline — builders follow a list, but don't change their behaviour around verifying material composition.

iii) It limits scalability — every new product must be individually assessed by and expert

iv) It weakens future mandate readiness — AO risks being seen as recommending preferences rather than enforcing requirements.

AO material standard (future state)

Australian Open Temporary Venue Overlay Material Standard (example)

Target materials must meet:

  • Recyclable through nominated processors OR
  • FSC / PEFC certified AND untreated above H2 OR
  • Use water based paints
  • Ply/ particleboard must be E0 / E1 AND accepted by local recycler
  • Avoid MDF as it is not recyclable

PLUS

Supplier must provide:

  • Technical data sheet
  • Resin composition
  • End-of-life pathway

Material constraint
MDF — unrecyclable, yet irreplaceable at its price point
No AU company accepts MDF. Formaldehyde resin makes it unrecoverable. Yet nothing else delivers its finish and price — making it the default substrate across the industry.
✕ No AU recycler ✕ No direct replacement
→ Limit use. Test alternatives: Compact Laminate, Melamine. Accept this is a multi-year shift
8
Commercial friction
Materials are where contractors make margin
Changing material sourcing directly impacts profit models.
→ Consider offsetting via reduced overall build costs
9
Technical constraint
High-res facade graphics require composites with no circular equivalent
ACP bonded to structural ply — the dominant branded facade approach — creates unrecoverable composites. Surface systems are the highest-waste, lowest-circularity category.
✕ No silver bullet
→ Distinguish evergreen vs annual graphic elements. Test new materials like fabrics with friendlies — TES, Greenspec, JTDodd, SeekR

Why these measures — and why in this order
  • AO27's hardest measure is executive budget sign-off (Risk 1, priority 25). Without an agreed cost uplift range, RFP responses can come back and TA can't action them. This must be resolved before the RFP goes out.
  • GSO and ballpark as the AO27 focus zone is grounded in Risk 3 and Finding 11 — optional materials drive low adoption. The program needs at least one zone with a fully controlled, end-to-end circular outcome to build AO28 proof points.
  • The shift from product list to AO Standard is an AO29 measure, not AO27. Risk 10 and Finding 2 both flag that a product-led approach is fit for transition but cannot be a permanent control. Years 1–2 build the evidence to earn that shift.
  • The accountability column escalates deliberately — year 1: clauses in RFP; year 2: real cost consequences for non-compliance; year 3: mandatory enforcement. This is the only path through the free-waste-bins incentive problem (Risk 6).
