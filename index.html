Summary
Scope: Current state materials will be benchmarked and mapped to viable closed-loop alternatives suitable for precinct long-term (evergreen) applications. The target responsible materials will detail: a) A specific product spec (e.g. MDF 18mm, sheet size) b) evergreen precinct applications (e.g. fascades) c) What it replaces d) Cost comparison to current state materials and Lifespan ROSI
A comparative business case will be modelled, with clear recommendations embedded into RFP specifications.
Primary focus: Precinct designers and agencies - the precinct RFP is the mechanism for change

What is known

i) Budget & cost resistance: feedback reflects that responsible alternatives have not been supported due to budgets
ii) Effort & time constraints: Circular design requires more resources and time than 'build & bin' approach. Limited bump out timeframe is a hard constraint to find time to disassemble
iii) Stakeholder alignment gap: Disconnect between partners, agencies, designers and builders, with few including materials recovery in designs and limited communication of target materials requirements
?

What is unknown

At this outset the adoption approach is not clear.

Q Will the materials recommendations be optional or mandatory
Q How will agencies/ builders report they have used a material for an evergreen design element
Q What is the lifecycle process - how will evergeen assets be procured, reported, tracked, stored
Q How will TA source segregate for closed-loop materials - relevant if flooring in scope and single use but recovered
